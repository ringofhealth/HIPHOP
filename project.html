She Got Game, The Documentary

Whether they’re rocking it out on their home consoles or portable gaming devices, designing the next hit RPG or competing in the Major League Gaming Championship, women are building a gaming world of their own. And yet their interests and achievements are still largely under-represented. We're interested in examining how gaming fits into women's lives socially and professionally. We also hope to learn about the particular challenges these women face and how they've built a community for themselves.

She Got Game explores uncharted territory through the eyes of an aspiring young gamer. This quirky and dynamic documentary will feature interviews with corporate and indie developers, as well as hardcore and casual gamers. We’ll also lead you behind the scenes of major cosplay events and competitions to uncover some of the mysteries surrounding the hardcore fan scene.

Faq:

What exactly are you going to do?

First, we plan to deliver in-depth coverage of exclusive events and to conduct visually and intellectually stimulating interviews with some of the key contributors to video game culture. We will also meet newcomers and fans.
Second, we hope to integrate sketches, parodies and other little gems that help our documentary reflect the vibrant gaming spirit.

What kind of topics are you going to cover?

  • Influential fictional and non-fictional female characters in video games
  • Misconceptions of the female video gamer
  • Women as casual, hardcore or competitive gamers
  • Sexuality in gaming
  • Rewards and challenges of working in the gaming industry
  • Fan culture, including cosplay and video game music
  • The relationship between fantasy and reality
  • Social groups and communities formed around gaming
  • Experiences of women who work as conceptual artists, composers, programmers, writers, designers, producers and more
  • The role of video games as a stress reliever
  • Fan culture and cosplay
  • The fine line between fantasy and reality
  • Men's perspectives on women and gaming
  • Game development and social responsibility
  • How do video games affect women's perception of themselves?
  • Women who really, really hate video games
  • Do video games make women more violent?
  • Can video games disrupt lives and relationships?
  • Do women tend to play different video games from men? Are they interested in games for different reasons? If so, why? What are those reasons?
  • What does the future of game development look like? How can we progress, both technologically and socially? How do we diversify the group of developers and players?
